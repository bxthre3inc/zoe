# Affiliate Model Analysis: Unified vs. Separate Roles

## The Unified Model (What We Spec'd)

### ✅ Why It Works

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Recruitment leverage** | Every affiliate becomes a potential marketer AND cash point — doubles your surface area for growth |
| **Geographic density** | High-cash affiliates naturally cluster where players are; they become local acquisition hubs |
| **Trust transfer** | Players who trust "their guy" for cash also trust their game recommendations |
| **Operational simplicity** | One onboarding flow, one dashboard, one commission structure to manage |
| **Lower per-transaction cost** | You're already paying the affiliate — no separate CPA for referrals from cash players |

### ⚠️ Where It Gets Messy

| Risk | Mitigation in Spec |
|------|-------------------|
| **Cash-first affiliates ignore marketing** | ACS weights marketing at 35% — forces balance |
| **Marketing-only affiliates feel shorted** | Tier bonuses give Silver/Gold floor guarantees even with zero cash |
| **One bad actor poisons both pools** | Bonding/insurance required; fraud affects both revenue streams |
| **Complex reconciliation** | Daily cash-to-credit matching; photo verification required |
| **Regulatory scrutiny** | Cash + gaming = MSB (money services business) triggers in most jurisdictions |

### 🔴 The Big One: Regulatory

**Cash-in-person + online gaming = likely MSB registration** in the US.

This means:
- FinCEN registration
- State-by-state money transmitter licenses
- AML program requirements
- Regular audits

**This is the primary risk of the unified model.** It's not a technical problem — it's a compliance cost that can run $500K–$2M annually depending on states.

## Alternative Models

### Option A: Fully Separate (Traditional)

**Cash Entities** | **Marketing Affiliates**
---|---
Licensed check-cashers, convenience stores, etc. | Online-only referral programs
Strict MSB compliance, bonding, insurance | Simple CPA or rev-share
High overhead, limited scale | Low touch, high volume
Transaction fees (3-5%) | No physical presence required

**Pros:** Clean regulatory separation; market it as "buy credits at participating retailers"
**Cons:** Lose the network effect; cash entities don't market; marketers don't accept cash

---

### Option B: Franchise Model

Affiliates are **independent franchisees** with:
- Exclusive territory rights
- Required marketing spend (enforced)
- Branded physical locations
- Revenue share instead of profit-share

**Pros:** More control over brand; legal separation from corporate
**Cons:** Complex franchise law (FTC regulations); higher barrier to entry for affiliates

---

### Option C: Hybrid — "Cash Partners" + "Marketing Affiliates"

Keep them functionally separate but in one system:

| Role | Cash | Marketing | Commission |
|------|------|-----------|------------|
| **Cash Partner** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No | 1% of deposits processed |
| **Marketing Affiliate** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes | Rev-share (20-30% of referred player losses) |
| **Hybrid Affiliate** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | Profit-share (as spec'd) |

**Pros:** Flexibility — some partners only want cash, others only want links; still allows unified dashboard
**Cons:** More complex commission calculations; need to explain three paths

---

### Option D: Cash-Only, Skip Marketing Integration

Pure cash logistics play:
- Affiliates = cash acceptance points only
- Marketing handled entirely by paid ads, SEO, social
- Affiliates earn flat 1-2% of cash processed

**Pros:** Simplest model; no profit-sharing complexity
**Cons:** No organic network effects; you're paying for all acquisition

## My Assessment

| Criteria | Unified | Separate | Franchise | Hybrid |
|----------|---------|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Regulatory risk** | 🔴 High | 🟡 Medium | 🟡 Medium | 🟡 Medium |
| **Network effects** | 🟢 Strong | 🔴 Weak | 🟢 Strong | 🟢 Strong |
| **Operational complexity** | 🟡 Medium | 🟢 Low | 🔴 High | 🟡 Medium |
| **Scalability** | 🟢 High | 🟢 High | 🟡 Medium | 🟢 High |
| **Affiliate recruitment** | 🟢 Easy | 🟡 Medium | 🔴 Hard | 🟢 Easy |

## Recommendation

**The unified model works IF:**
1. You have legal counsel familiar with MSB/gaming crossover
2. You're prepared to spend $500K+ annually on compliance
3. You can secure insurance/bonding for cash-handling affiliates
4. Your market is geographically concentrated (easier to audit)

**Consider Option C (Hybrid) IF:**
1. You want to start simple with marketing-only affiliates
2. You want to pilot cash-in-person in one market before scaling
3. Regulatory clarity is still developing in your target states

**Consider Option D (Cash-Only) IF:**
1. This is a side experiment, not core to the business
2. You have a strong existing marketing engine (paid acquisition works)
3. You want to minimize legal exposure

---

## The Real Question

Are you building a **gaming platform that accepts cash** (unified model) or a **cash logistics network for gaming** (separate model)?

The unified model is defensible long-term. The separate model is safer short-term. The hybrid lets you test both.
