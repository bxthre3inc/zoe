The 1938 Compact Obligation

The Rio Grande Compact of 1938 legally binds Colorado, New Mexico, and Texas to deliver specific water volumes to the Rio Grande. For 87 years, this has been managed through reservoir releases and informal accounting.

Now, the system is collapsing.

The Threat: Agricultural Well Shutdowns

The State of Colorado faces a choice:

  1. Prove compact compliance with defensible water accounting data, OR
  2. Shut down agricultural wells to reduce consumption and meet downstream obligations

The State Engineer has already issued warnings: Without credible accounting, wells will be curtailed. This is not a fee increase. This is existential — 300,000 acres of irrigated agriculture in the San Luis Valley are at risk of immediate shutdown.

FarmSense as the Only Solution

Colorado needs:

  • Legally admissible water accounting (accepted by Water Court)

  • 1-meter resolution (defensible spatial granularity)

FarmSense is the only system that provides all four.

FarmSense is the only system with Water Court admissibility. The June 29, 2026 trial establishes precedent: FarmSense data is legally binding. Competitors would need years of litigation to achieve similar status.

FarmSense fields improve each other's accuracy through spatial interpolation. A 317,000-field mesh achieves centimetric precision impossible with isolated deployments. Competitors face a chicken-and-egg problem: need density for accuracy, need accuracy for adoption.
